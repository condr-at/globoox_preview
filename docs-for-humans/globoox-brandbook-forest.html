01 — Brand Essence

Who we are. What we believe.

Globoox exists to dismantle language as a barrier to human knowledge and literature. These principles are the foundation of every brand decision.

Mission

Make every story accessible to every reader.

We believe profound literature should not be locked behind a language. We translate thought, not merely words.

Vision

A world where no book remains unread.

7,000 spoken languages. Millions of untranslated works. Globoox closes that gap — one book at a time, one reader at a time.

Promise

Nuance preserved. Voice intact.

We never flatten a text. Our engine is trained on literary corpora so it understands register, rhythm, and cultural idiom.

Positioning

Literary translation, reimagined by AI.

Not a dictionary tool. Not a utility. A reading companion that respects both the author and the reader.

06 — Imagery & Illustration Style

Warm, literary, unhurried.

Globoox imagery evokes the intimacy of reading — warm light, analogue textures, the human act of encountering a story. Avoid sterile tech stock photography.

People reading

Natural light. Real environments. Non-posed. Evoke absorption in a story.

哲学
Poésie
قصة
Script overlays

Multiple writing systems layered — represents our core mission. Used in hero & decorative contexts.

Book spine motif

Signature visual element. Use in product illustrations, empty states & hero sections.

✓ Use
  • Warm, slightly desaturated photography (film-like tones)
  • Analogue textures: paper grain, linen, aged parchment
  • Multiple scripts together as visual motifs
  • Illustrations with book spine / page / light references
  • People of varied backgrounds engaged in reading
  • Natural settings: libraries, cafés, reading chairs, trains
✗ Avoid
  • Over-saturated or cold-filtered stock photography
  • Purple/blue "AI aesthetic" gradients
  • Generic tech imagery (servers, circuits, robots)
  • Posed, corporate-feeling photography
  • Neon glow effects or futuristic cyber styles
  • Clipart, flat generic icons without character
07 — Motion & Animation

Motion that reads like prose — unhurried.

Animation should feel literary: deliberate, not frenetic. We favour smooth eases over snappy springs, and restrained choreography over constant movement.

Lift

Used on cards, book spines, and interactive tiles. Creates depth without drama.

cubic-bezier(.22,1,.36,1) · 600ms
translateY(-8px) + shadow

Fade

Page transitions, modal overlays, content reveals. Opacity only — no layout shift.

ease · 200–300ms
opacity 1 → 0.4

Grow

Buttons, icon affordances, CTAs. Subtle — never exceeds scale(1.15).

cubic-bezier(.34,1.56,.64,1) · 300ms
scale(1) → scale(1.12)
Easing Reference
Name Value Use for
Entrance cubic-bezier(.22,1,.36,1) Elements arriving on screen
Exit cubic-bezier(.36,.07,.19,.97) Elements leaving screen
Bounce cubic-bezier(.34,1.56,.64,1) CTA, grow interactions
Standard ease Opacity, colour, subtle transitions
Principles

Restrained

Not every element should move. Animate to aid comprehension, not to impress.

Purposeful

Motion should direct attention or confirm action, never distract from content.

Accessible

Always respect prefers-reduced-motion. Remove decorative animations entirely.
